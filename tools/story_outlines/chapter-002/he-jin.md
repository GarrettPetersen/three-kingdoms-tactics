# Chapter 002 POV Outline - He Jin

- Chapter title: 002-第二回-張翼德怒鞭督郵，何國舅謀誅宦豎
- Source chapter file: `三國志演義_章節/002-第二回-張翼德怒鞭督郵，何國舅謀誅宦豎.md`
- Source span: from `中平六年，夏四月，靈帝病篤` through Cao Cao laughing at He Jin's summons plan.

## Story Title
- EN: The Regent and the Knives
- ZH: 国舅与刀锋

## Teaser
- EN: Emperor Ling dies with the succession contested. He Jin survives a eunuch trap, places Liu Bian on the throne, and then hesitates between mercy, palace pressure, and a purge that may save or doom the Han court.
- ZH: 灵帝病逝，储位暗争。何进避过宦官陷阱，扶立刘辩即位，却在太后、外戚、士人和宦官之间摇摆，迟迟不能定下清君侧之策。

## Route Premise
- He Jin is not a battlefield hero route. His chapter should play as court crisis management: audience scenes, council choices, palace-security pressure, and map decisions around Luoyang.
- The source-faithful path can be completely combat-free. Branches alter who trusts He Jin, how publicly force is displayed, and what political flags carry into chapter 3, but the player must be able to follow the novel's event order without invented battles.

## Full Scene Arc

1) [narrative] Lingdi's sickbed and succession crisis.
   - Setting: imperial bedchamber / inner palace.
   - Source beats: Emperor Ling is dying; He Jin is summoned to discuss succession; He Jin's rise is explained through Empress He and Prince Bian; Prince Xie is favored through Wang Meiren and Dong Taikou.
   - Gameplay purpose: establish He Jin's legitimacy and the rival succession line.
   - Branch option:
     - "Enter at once" increases boldness but risks Jian Shuo's trap.
     - "Question the summons" gives Pan Yin a chance to warn him more cleanly.

2) [narrative] Jian Shuo's palace-gate trap.
   - Setting: palace gate / covered approach.
   - Source beats: Jian Shuo plans to kill He Jin before Prince Xie can be blocked; Pan Yin warns He Jin not to enter; He Jin retreats to his residence.
   - Gameplay: dialogue and timing pressure only; He Jin never has to fight his way out.
   - Required result: Pan Yin's warning prevents He Jin from entering the trap.
   - Branch effect: if the player delayed in scene 1, Pan Yin starts closer and gives a cleaner escape.

3) [narrative] He Jin's residence council after the failed trap.
   - Setting: general-in-chief residence / military council hall.
   - Source beats: He Jin calls ministers and wants to kill the eunuchs; Cao Cao warns that eunuch power is deeply rooted and secrecy matters; He Jin dismisses Cao Cao as young and ignorant.
   - Branch option:
     - "Listen to Cao Cao" records a caution flag; improves later advice, lowers Yuan Shao approval.
     - "Dismiss him" follows the novel tone; raises Yuan Shao approval, lowers Cao Cao trust.

4) [narrative] Secret death of Lingdi and the forged summons.
   - Setting: same residence, urgent messenger arrival.
   - Source beats: Pan Yin reports Lingdi is dead; Jian Shuo and the Ten Attendants conceal the death and send a false order to bring He Jin in; Cao Cao says to settle the succession first, then punish the traitors.
   - Gameplay purpose: move from survival to initiative.
   - Required result: He Jin chooses to enter with force and enthrone Prince Bian.

5) [narrative / ceremony] Enter the palace and enthrone Liu Bian.
   - Setting: imperial palace corridors / coffin hall.
   - Source beats: Yuan Shao volunteers 5,000 elite troops; He Jin enters with He Yong, Xun You, Zheng Tai, and others; Liu Bian is raised before Lingdi's coffin.
   - Gameplay: ceremonial procession and council staging; Yuan Shao's troops are political pressure and protection, not a playable battle.
   - Branch objective:
     - Enter with strict discipline for stronger court legitimacy.
     - Enter with a heavier show of force for higher eunuch hostility.

6) [narrative event] Yuan Shao seizes Jian Shuo.
   - Setting: imperial garden shadows.
   - Source beats: Yuan Shao enters to arrest Jian Shuo; Jian Shuo flees into the imperial garden and is killed by Guo Sheng; Jian Shuo's forbidden troops submit.
   - Gameplay: event scene or report; Jian Shuo should die by Guo Sheng's hand, preserving the source.
   - Branch effect:
     - If He Jin ordered strict discipline, more forbidden troops surrender.
     - If He Jin ordered vengeance, more palace fear and eunuch hostility are recorded.

7) [narrative choice] The first purge debate.
   - Setting: palace council chamber.
   - Source beats: Yuan Shao urges killing all eunuchs; Zhang Rang and others beg Empress He; Empress He reminds He Jin that the eunuchs helped raise the He family; He Jin spares all except Jian Shuo's faction.
   - Main branch:
     - "Accept the Empress' plea" follows the novel, raises palace-family loyalty, preserves eunuch power.
     - "Prepare a limited purge" lets the player mark named eunuch ringleaders, raising reformer support but provoking He Miao and the palace.
   - Hard constraint: no branch fully destroys the Ten Attendants here.

8) [narrative] Dong Taikou's countercourt.
   - Setting: inner palace banquet / rival curtain court.
   - Source beats: Dong Taikou empowers Prince Xie, Dong Zhong, and Zhang Rang; Empress He invites Dong Taikou to a banquet and urges women to withdraw from court politics; they trade insults over Wang Meiren and the He family's butcher origins.
   - Gameplay purpose: show the court splitting into He and Dong factions.
   - Branch option:
     - "Counsel restraint to Empress He" reduces bloodshed later.
     - "Encourage her to strike first" increases He faction control but worsens legitimacy.

9) [narrative / order scene] Surround Dong Zhong's residence.
   - Setting: Luoyang noble residence / military street.
   - Source beats: He Jin has ministers force Dong Taikou back to Hejian and sends forbidden troops to surround Dong Zhong's residence; Dong Zhong kills himself.
   - Gameplay: orders, messenger reports, and a residence exterior scene; no playable siege is required.
   - Branch effect:
     - Handle the seals cleanly: He faction authority rises.
     - Make a harsher public display: rumors against He Jin grow.

10) [narrative] Poison at Hejian and the eunuchs' bribery net.
    - Setting: He Jin residence at night / messenger reports.
    - Source beats: Dong Taikou is poisoned at Hejian; He Jin claims illness and withdraws; Zhang Rang and Duan Gui bribe He Miao and Lady Wuyang to protect them through Empress He.
    - Gameplay purpose: consequences of offscreen political violence.
    - Branch effect: if the player previously counseled restraint, He Jin can deny the murder more credibly; if not, rumors are sharper.

11) [narrative council] Yuan Shao's second warning.
    - Setting: He Jin headquarters.
    - Source beats: Yuan Shao warns that Zhang Rang and Duan Gui are spreading rumors and must be killed now; He Jin says he will consider it; informants leak the discussion to Zhang Rang; He Miao persuades Empress He that He Jin is too bloodthirsty.
    - Branch option:
      - "Move tonight" unlocks a planned palace-entry tactical scene but Empress He blocks it.
      - "Seek the Empress' approval" follows the novel and stalls.
    - Hard constraint: He Jin cannot get Empress He's approval.

12) [narrative] Empress He refuses the purge.
    - Setting: inner audience chamber.
    - Source beats: He Jin asks to kill the eunuchs; Empress He says eunuchs have always managed the forbidden palace and it dishonors the late emperor to kill old servants immediately after his death.
    - Gameplay purpose: place He Jin in the trap of family loyalty versus political necessity.
    - Branch response:
      - "Yield" raises He family loyalty, lowers reformer trust.
      - "Argue" raises reformer trust, but He Miao grows hostile.

13) [map + strategic choice] Summon outside commanders.
    - Setting: Luoyang map / courier routes.
    - Source beats: Yuan Shao proposes summoning regional commanders with troops to force the Empress to comply; He Jin approves and sends orders to the provinces.
    - Gameplay: strategic map choice of which summons to prioritize first. This can seed chapter 3.
    - Required summons:
      - Dong Zhuo receives a path toward Luoyang.
      - Other "heroes and men of power" can be represented as route markers.
    - Branch effect: the order/tone of summons affects chapter 3 deployment and who arrives with confidence.

14) [narrative] Chen Lin and Cao Cao object.
    - Setting: He Jin council hall, final scene.
    - Source beats: Chen Lin warns that calling outside armies is like handing the weapon away; He Jin mocks him as timid; Cao Cao laughs and says the matter is easy if handled properly.
    - Ending: chapter card lands on Cao Cao's laugh and the unresolved question, matching the source's "listen to the next chapter" cliffhanger.
    - Branch echo:
      - If He Jin listened to Cao Cao earlier, Cao Cao's final line is sharper but less contemptuous.
      - If He Jin dismissed him, Cao Cao's laugh reads as open judgment.

## Branch State To Carry Forward

## Combat / Interaction Notes

- There is no direct He Jin combat in this source span. A faithful playthrough should be allowed to complete the route without any tactical battle.
- Do not implement Palace Gate Escape, Coffin Hall Escort, Imperial Garden Arrest, or Dong Zhong Residence as required battles for the source-faithful path.
- Usable interactions here are dialogue choices, council responses, ceremonial staging, map/courier priorities, and public-versus-private order tone.
- Branches should affect trust, legitimacy, hostility, rumors, and chapter 3 setup. They should not insert a battle or let He Jin avert the succession crisis.
- If we later add any tactics map in this route, label it clearly as adaptation-only and keep the novel-exact route available.
